## Regulatory Landscape and Industry Reaction

The NGB has proactively issued directives to enforce strict compliance following the SCA ruling, which it strongly welcomed. This has led to the removal of casino games from platforms previously offering them under a sports betting license.

The Casino Association of South Africa (CASA) actively championed this legal clarification, taking the matter to court to oppose the "grey area" operations by bookmakers that offered casino games. Their stance was that such activities conflated lawful betting with prohibited interactive gambling.

Conversely, the South African Bookmakers Association (SABA) has pushed back against the NGB's interpretation, particularly regarding directives on Remote Gambling Servers (RGS). SABA argues that the NGB's approach risks misinterpreting existing legislation, maintaining that a bet or wager offered by a bookmaker does not constitute a gambling game and, therefore, is not an interactive game.

The Internet Service Providers' Association (ISPA) has urged South African consumers to avoid illegal offshore online gambling platforms due to the risks of losing winnings and facing unfair betting conditions.
