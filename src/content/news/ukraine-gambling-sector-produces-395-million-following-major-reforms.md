## Context & Background

The growth observed in the Ukrainian gambling sector is a direct consequence of a shift from an unregulated black market to a licensed and controlled environment. This transition has involved a series of legislative and operational changes designed to professionalize the industry. A core aim of these reforms is to ensure safer gambling practices and enhance player protection. For instance, a ban on cold-calling or texting unknown recipients by gambling organizers has been implemented, directly addressing unsolicited marketing.

Discussions around player protection have gained prominence, notably highlighted by a petition initiated by Ukrainian soldier Pavlo Petrychenko. This petition brought to light concerns regarding gambling addiction among military personnel during martial law, leading to calls for restrictions on online gambling for soldiers. This indicates a concentrated effort to protect vulnerable groups within the regulatory framework.

The industry's contributions extend beyond revenue, actively supporting the state budget and social initiatives, including financing the Ukrainian Armed Forces and humanitarian programs during the ongoing full-scale invasion by Russia. This conflict has also accelerated the shift towards online gambling platforms. Further refining regulatory oversight, KRAIL, the former regulatory body, has been replaced by PlayCity, signaling the government's commitment to strengthening sector governance. The resumption of lottery licensing after a 12-year hiatus is also anticipated to create new revenue streams for the state.
