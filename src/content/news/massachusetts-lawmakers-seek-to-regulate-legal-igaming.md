# Massachusetts Lawmakers Advance iGaming Regulation in 2026

Massachusetts lawmakers are actively reviewing legislation that could establish formal **Massachusetts iGaming Regulation 2026**, aiming to legalize online casino gaming within the state. Two bills, House Bill 332 and Senate Bill 235, are currently under consideration, proposing a framework designed to generate significant tax revenue and protect consumers from unregulated online gambling.

This legislative push follows the successful launch of online sports betting in Massachusetts in early 2023, which has exceeded initial revenue expectations. The proposed iGaming expansion represents a further step in broadening the state's regulated gambling market.

## Proposed Framework for Massachusetts iGaming Regulation 2026

The core of the proposed **Massachusetts iGaming Regulation 2026** involves a structured licensing system. Under the current drafts of House Bill 332 and Senate Bill 235, the state's three existing casinos would be permitted to partner with up to two online operators each. The Massachusetts Gaming Commission would also have the authority to issue additional licenses beyond these casino partnerships, expanding potential market access.

For operators, a license would cost $5 million and be valid for a five-year period. Successful applicants would then be subject to a 20% tax rate on their earnings. The state projects this initiative could generate up to $275 million in annual tax revenue, providing a substantial boost to public funds. A key objective of this legislation is also to safeguard players by directing them away from illegal, offshore gambling sites towards a regulated, secure environment.

## Context and Industry Reaction

The move to regulate online casino gaming aligns Massachusetts with a growing trend among other U.S. states. Neighboring states like Connecticut and Rhode Island have already legalized iGaming, with Maine and several other jurisdictions, including Maryland, Indiana, Wyoming, and New York, actively exploring similar legislation.

Industry stakeholders largely support the proposed **MA online casino legislation**. Representatives from major operators like DraftKings and FanDuel have voiced strong backing. David Prestwood, Government Affairs Manager for DraftKings, highlighted that iGaming could provide significant tax revenue and enhance citizen protection by regulating activities already occurring in illegal markets. FanDuel's James Hartman echoed this, emphasizing the substantial revenue already being generated by unlicensed online casinos. National advocacy groups, such as the Sports Betting Alliance, also advocate for regulation, citing economic advantages and consumer safety.

Conversely, the initiative faces notable opposition.
