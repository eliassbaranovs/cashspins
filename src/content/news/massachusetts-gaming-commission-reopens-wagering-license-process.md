# Massachusetts Gaming Commission Reopens Sports Betting Licenses 2026

The Massachusetts Gaming Commission (MGC) unanimously voted on April 10, 2026, to reopen the application process for sports betting licenses, a decision influenced by a formal inquiry from global operator bet365. This action, occurring two years after the initial launch of online sports betting in the state, signals a potential expansion of the market and increased competition for players in Massachusetts.

## Key Details of the MGC Decision

Four untethered online sports betting licenses and several tethered licenses remain available in Massachusetts. The MGC's decision to reopen the application process provides an opportunity for new operators, potentially including bet365, to enter the market. This move comes as the MGC continues to address the proliferation of prediction markets, which the commission does not support.

## Context and Background

Massachusetts launched its online sports betting market in March 2023. Since then, the MGC has actively regulated the sector, including issuing fines to major sports betting operators for compliance failures, such as offering prohibited wager types. The commission has also pursued legal action and issued warnings against unregulated prediction market operators, reinforcing its commitment to a structured and compliant betting environment.

The industry's interest in the Massachusetts market remains strong, evidenced by bet365's formal request. MGC Chair Jordan Maynard has expressed a positive view of regulated operators seeking entry, contrasting this with the ongoing issues presented by unregulated prediction markets. However, some commissioners, such as Eileen O'Brien, have voiced caution regarding market saturation and the necessity for economic analysis before new licenses are granted.
