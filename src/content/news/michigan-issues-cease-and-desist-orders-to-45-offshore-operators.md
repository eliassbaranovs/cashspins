# Michigan Issues Cease and Desist Orders to 45 Offshore Operators

**LANSING, MI – April 9, 2026** – Michigan has issued cease and desist orders to 45 offshore online gambling operators, effective April 8, 2026. This decisive action by the state targets unlicensed entities to prevent their operation within Michigan's borders, reinforcing the integrity of its regulated iGaming market. The move protects players from risks associated with platforms operating outside state oversight, such as inadequate consumer protection and unverified fair play standards.

## Key Details of Michigan's Enforcement Action

The Michigan Gaming Control Board (MGCB) initiated this enforcement against a substantial number of offshore online gambling operators. The issuance of cease and desist orders directly prohibits these entities from offering their services to Michigan residents. This proactive measure underscores Michigan's commitment to maintaining a secure and regulated environment for online gambling, aiming to channel all iGaming activity through state-licensed operators. Such enforcement actions are increasingly common in regulated U.S. states as they seek to safeguard their legal markets and player interests.

## Context and Background of iGaming Enforcement

Michigan's crackdown on offshore operators is part of a sustained and aggressive pattern of enforcement by the MGCB. This stance positions Michigan as a leading state in the U.S. for pursuing illegal and unlicensed gambling activities. A similar push against iGaming giant Bovada in May 2025 reportedly prompted similar actions from other states, highlighting Michigan's influence in this regulatory space. The MGCB, under Executive Director Henry Williams, has consistently articulated its commitment to "relentless, decisive action to prevent unlicensed operators from targeting Michigan residents and ensure that all gaming is conducted lawfully and responsibly." Williams has previously stated that "Illegal online gambling sites operate without oversight, putting players at risk and undermining Michigan's secure, regulated marketplace."

Beyond enforcement, Michigan actively educates consumers through its "Play It Smart, Michigan" campaign, which includes a "Legal vs. Illegal Gaming" section on its website.
