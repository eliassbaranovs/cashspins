# Florida's HB 189 Advances to Combat Illegal Online Gambling with Stricter Penalties

Florida's House Bill 189 advanced through legislative stages in 2026, aiming to significantly strengthen enforcement against illegal online gambling by expanding criminal penalties to third-degree felonies. The proposed legislation sought to broadly define internet gambling to include online games of chance, intending to protect state revenue and consumer interests. Despite its progression, HB 189 ultimately failed to pass during the 2026 legislative session, meaning its proposed changes did not become law.

## Florida HB 189 Advances: Key Details of the Proposed Online Gambling Law

House Bill 189, introduced in Florida, aimed to overhaul the state's approach to illegal online gambling. A central provision of the bill was the expansion of criminal penalties for illegal gambling, elevating them to third-degree felonies. This change would have imposed more severe consequences for operators and participants in unauthorized online gambling activities.

The proposed legislation broadly defined internet gambling, encompassing online games of chance. This expansive definition intended to cover a wide array of digital platforms and activities currently operating outside Florida's regulatory framework. A primary objective of HB 189 was to protect state revenue streams and uphold the integrity of the Seminole compact, which grants the Seminole Tribe exclusive rights to certain gaming operations.

Additionally, the bill sought to strengthen the oversight capabilities of the Florida Gaming Control Commission. This commission would have received greater powers to investigate and prosecute illegal gambling operations. HB 189 also included provisions clarifying the legality of fantasy sports contests. These provisions aimed to provide a defined legal space for participants and operators in that sector.

## Context: Why Florida's HB 189 Aimed to Reform Online Gambling

House Bill 189 received support from key figures who stressed its importance in combating illicit gambling activities. Bill sponsor Rep. They cited analyses, including one from the American Gaming Association. This analysis pointed to significant revenue losses attributable to the illicit gaming market.

The push for HB 189 reflected a broader effort to crack down on unauthorized online platforms that operate without state oversight. However, the bill also faced criticism from several lawmakers and industry groups. The Amusement Machine Association of Florida, for example, expressed concerns regarding the bill's broad language and potential unintended consequences, particularly concerning existing statutes and the definition of "amusement devices."
