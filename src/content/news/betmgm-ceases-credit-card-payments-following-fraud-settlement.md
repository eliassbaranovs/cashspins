# BetMGM Ceases Credit Card Payments Following Pennsylvania Fraud Settlement

BetMGM will no longer process credit card payments for online gaming, effective on or around April 1, 2026. This policy change follows a $100,000 fine imposed by the Pennsylvania Gaming Control Board (PGCB) due to the operator's failure to prevent fraudulent activities involving stolen identities. The move positions BetMGM alongside other major operators like FanDuel and DraftKings, which have already discontinued credit card payments, signaling a broader industry shift towards enhanced security and compliance in online transactions. This change directly impacts players, who will need to adapt to alternative deposit methods.

## Key Details of the PGCB Fine

The Pennsylvania Gaming Control Board issued the $100,000 fine against BetMGM after an investigation revealed instances where individuals utilized stolen or fabricated identities to gamble and subsequently withdraw funds. This enforcement action underscores the regulator's focus on robust Know-Your-Customer (KYC) protocols and fraud prevention within the iGaming sector. BetMGM has confirmed its plan to phase out credit card usage as a direct result of this settlement.

## Industry Context and Regulatory Scrutiny

The decision by BetMGM to halt credit card payments for online gaming is not an isolated event. FanDuel and DraftKings, two other prominent online gaming operators, have already implemented similar bans. Industry analysts suggest that this collective action serves as a preemptive measure to improve regulatory standing and enhance Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) optics. The iGaming industry has experienced a significant increase in fraud, with online gambling fraud doubling in the last two years, from 0.70% in 2023 to 1.39% in Q1 2025. This rise is attributed to sophisticated fraud networks, automation tools, and AI-driven attacks, placing increased pressure on operators to strengthen their security measures.

The PGCB has previously fined BetMGM. In 2025, the operator received a $260,905 penalty from the PGCB for allowing self-excluded individuals to place bets online, highlighting ongoing challenges with compliance across various operational areas.

Internationally, the United Kingdom implemented a ban on credit card gambling in April 2020. This policy has been positively received by consumers, although its long-term effectiveness in preventing problem gamblers from accessing other forms of credit remains under evaluation. Australia also enacted a ban on credit cards for online wagering in 2024, which showed minimal impact on heavy gamblers, who largely shifted to other transaction accounts.

## What This Means for Players Regarding BetMGM Credit Card Payments 2026

Online casino players on BetMGM platforms will no longer be able to use credit cards for deposits. This necessitates a shift to alternative payment methods such as debit cards, ACH transfers, bank-linked accounts, and other digital payment options.

From a responsible gambling perspective, this change is largely viewed as a positive development. It prevents players from wagering with borrowed money, which can lead to high-interest debt and exacerbate problem gambling behaviors. Players will also avoid the cash advance fees and higher interest rates often associated with using credit cards for gambling transactions. However, some research suggests that while direct credit card use is prevented, problem gamblers might seek other forms of borrowed money or non-traditional credit sources.

## Financial Impact and Future Outlook

While BetMGM declined to comment directly on the sanction, the company confirmed its plan to phase out credit card usage. Industry analysts generally believe that the financial impact on operators will be minimal in the long term. Most deposit volume is expected to shift to debit cards, ACH transfers, and digital wallets. Analysts also suggest that credit cards constitute a relatively small percentage of deposits, often used by new or casual bettors. This policy change is therefore anticipated to have a limited effect on overall revenue, while bolstering security and compliance. The cessation of BetMGM credit card payments in 2026 aligns with a growing industry trend towards more secure and responsible financial practices.
