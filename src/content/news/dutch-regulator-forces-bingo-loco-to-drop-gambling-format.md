# Dutch Regulator Compels Bingo Loco to Cease Gambling Format in Netherlands

The Dutch gambling regulator, Kansspelautoriteit (KSA), has compelled entertainment provider Bingo Loco to cease operating its events in a gambling format within the Netherlands, effective April 3, 2026. This enforcement action by the **Bingo Loco Dutch Regulator 2026** underscores the KSA's stringent stance on unauthorized gambling activities and its commitment to ensuring entertainment events comply with national gambling laws.

The ruling directly affects the format of entertainment events that may resemble gambling, ensuring that all operators adhere to the legal framework governing games of chance in the Netherlands. For players attending these events, the immediate consequence is a change in the participatory structure.

## KSA Enforces Strict Compliance with Dutch Gambling Laws

The KSA's order mandates that Bingo Loco discontinue its gambling-style events, which previously involved elements of chance and prize distribution. This action is a direct enforcement of compliance with **Dutch gambling laws**, reinforcing the regulator's authority over both online and offline activities perceived to be gambling. The KSA's stringent approach to unauthorized gambling activities aims to protect consumers and maintain the integrity of the regulated market.

In response to the KSA's warnings, Bingo Loco promptly redesigned its events to comply with Dutch regulations, converting them into music quizzes. This adaptation allows the company to continue operating in the Dutch market while aligning with local legal requirements, demonstrating a willingness to adjust to regulatory demands.
