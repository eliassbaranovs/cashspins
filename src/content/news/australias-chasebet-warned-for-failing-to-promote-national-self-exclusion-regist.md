# Chasebet ACMA Warning 2026 Issued for BetStop Promotion Failure

Australian wagering operator Harris Bookmaking Pty Ltd, trading as Chasebet, received a formal warning from the Australian Communications and Media Authority (ACMA) on April 15, 2026. The Chasebet ACMA warning was issued for failing to adequately promote BetStop, Australia's National Self-Exclusion Register, in its marketing emails and on its website.

This enforcement action underscores the ACMA's continued focus on ensuring compliance with responsible gambling obligations across the Australian online wagering sector. Wagering providers are legally required to promote BetStop in all electronic communications and on their websites.

## Key Details of the Non-Compliance

The ACMA identified Chasebet's non-compliance in specific marketing emails and on its official website. Following notification from the regulator, Chasebet rectified the issue, bringing its operations into line with the mandated promotional requirements.

BetStop allows individuals to self-exclude from all licensed Australian online and phone wagering services through a single registration. This national register is a critical tool within Australia's framework for **[Responsible Gambling](/responsible-gambling/)**.

## Context of BetStop and Regulatory Enforcement

BetStop's purpose is to protect vulnerable individuals by preventing them from accessing gambling services if they choose to self-exclude. When a player registers with BetStop, wagering providers are legally obligated to close their accounts, block new account creation, and cease all marketing communications.

Failures in promoting BetStop directly undermine these protections. Such breaches can expose self-excluded individuals to continued gambling content, potentially exacerbating gambling harm. The ACMA's enforcement actions aim to uphold these safeguards.
