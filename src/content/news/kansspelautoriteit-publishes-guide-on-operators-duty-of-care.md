# Kansspelautoriteit Issues Stricter Duty of Care Guidance for Dutch Operators in 2026

The Kansspelautoriteit (KSA), the Dutch gambling regulator, published new guidance on operator duty of care on April 7, 2026. This guidance aims to significantly enhance player protection and responsible gambling practices for online gambling license holders in the Netherlands.

The new measures are mandatory for all online gambling operators holding a license in the Dutch market. This KSA guidance is predicated on two recent studies shared by the regulator, indicating a commitment to evidence-based policy. The regulatory update is expected to lead to stricter responsible gaming tools and interventions for players across the Netherlands.

## Key Details of the KSA Guidance

The core of this regulatory update mandates that online gambling operators implement more stringent measures to protect players and prevent gambling-related harm. For players, this translates into more robust responsible gaming tools and direct interventions designed to ensure a safer gambling environment. This focus on **responsible gambling Netherlands** represents an evolution in regulatory oversight.

Operators are now explicitly required to advise players who exhibit signs of problem gambling to register with Cruks, the national self-exclusion register. Should a player decline this advice, the operator must report the refusal to the KSA. The regulator retains the authority to subsequently register the player in Cruks involuntarily.

Furthermore, personal interviews are now mandated when there is a suspicion of excessive gambling or addiction. The new KSA guidance provides detailed instructions on the timing, contact methods, and content of these conversations, aiming for more effective intervention. Players can anticipate stricter monitoring of their behavior, with operators required to intervene within one hour of detecting signals of excessive play.

Financial deposit limits have been reinforced: €700 per month for players aged 24 and older, and €300 per month for those aged 18-24. Affordability checks are now a prerequisite for players seeking to set higher limits than these thresholds. The overall objective of this **online gambling duty of care** framework is to cultivate a safer playing environment and safeguard individuals from gambling-related harm.

## Context and Background

This updated **Kansspelautoriteit Duty of Care 2026** framework follows a period of active development and enforcement by the KSA. In December 2025, the regulator held a roundtable discussion where licensed operators provided feedback on their practical experiences and identified challenges in implementing duty of care measures. This collaborative approach indicates that the industry had input into the clarification process. Operators had previously encountered difficulties with low response rates during personal interviews and uncertainties regarding involuntary Cruks registrations, issues the new guidance directly addresses.

While concerns were raised that stricter regulations might inadvertently push players towards the black market, a KSA study conducted in early 2025 indicated no significant increase in players circumventing rules by creating multiple accounts. However, the gross gaming result for legal providers did experience a decline.

These developments align with the KSA's ongoing efforts to strengthen **Dutch gambling regulation**. In July 2024, the KSA established a dedicated "Duty of Care Online" department specifically to enhance the supervision of online gambling operators. Prior to this, in June 2024, the KSA amended its Responsible Gaming Policy, introducing mandatory real-time monitoring and financial deposit limits. The regulator has also consistently enforced advertising rules, including a ban on the use of role models in gambling advertisements, and has been proactive in combating illegal gambling activities, both online and in land-based establishments. Most recently, the KSA issued a notice to licensed sports betting operators, reminding them of their license limitations and addressing offerings that fell outside permitted sports betting categories.

## What This Means for Players

Online casino players in the Netherlands will experience direct effects from these enhanced **player protection Netherlands** measures. The new KSA guidance ensures a more proactive approach from operators in identifying and addressing potential gambling-related harm.

Players showing signs of problematic play will be directly advised to use the Cruks self-exclusion register. Refusal will trigger a report to the KSA, potentially leading to an involuntary registration, a significant step towards safeguarding vulnerable individuals.
