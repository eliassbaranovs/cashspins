# Ksa Warns Bingo Loco for Illegal Events in Netherlands, Reinforcing Regulatory Vigilance

The Dutch Gaming Authority (Ksa) issued a warning to Bingo Loco on April 2, 2026, for organizing bingo events in the Netherlands without the necessary legal permits. This action by the Ksa warns Bingo Loco, explicitly highlighting the regulator's commitment to strict adherence to licensing requirements and player protection within the Dutch gambling market.

Bingo Loco organized these events in the Netherlands without possessing a valid gambling license, classifying them as illegal under Dutch law. The Ksa's intervention underscores its vigilance against unlicensed gambling activities and its aim to protect players from the inherent risks associated with unregulated events. All operators active in the Netherlands must adhere to strict licensing requirements to ensure legal compliance and consumer safety.

## Key Details of the Enforcement Action

The warning to Bingo Loco serves as a direct consequence of its operation outside the established regulatory framework. The Ksa's mandate includes overseeing all forms of gambling to ensure fair play, transparency, and the protection of vulnerable individuals. By operating without a license, Bingo Loco bypassed these critical safeguards, creating an environment where participants lacked the consumer protections afforded by regulated activities.

## Context and Broader Regulatory Landscape

This enforcement action is consistent with the Ksa's proactive approach to combating illegal gambling and ensuring regulatory compliance across the Netherlands. The regulator has demonstrated a firm stance against unlicensed operations through various measures.

In November 2023, the Ksa shut down an illegal bingo operation in Dordrecht, confiscating prizes and noting the presence of minors. Further, in January 2025, a €200,000 penalty payment order was issued to "Baco's Zaalbingo" for repeatedly organizing and advertising illegal bingo via Facebook live streams. Similarly, in September 2024, fines were imposed on the owner of the "Luxurybingods" social media account for comparable unlicensed bingo activities. These instances collectively illustrate the Ksa's persistent efforts against illegal bingo operations.

Beyond bingo, the Ksa has also warned and fined influencers for promoting unlicensed gambling operators, particularly to younger audiences, reflecting a stricter stance on gambling advertising following reforms to the Remote Gambling Act. Warnings have also been issued to licensed operators regarding their adherence to anti-money laundering and counter-terrorist financing rules, demonstrating a comprehensive regulatory overview.

The Ksa has also expressed concerns about a potential increase in illegal online gambling activity. This follows the introduction of new player protection measures and deposit limits in October 2024, which, while reducing player losses for licensed operators, may inadvertently cause some players to seek out unlicensed sites. A Ksa study in 2025 further highlighted that risk analyses conducted by licensed online gambling operators are often inadequate in effectively protecting players.

Following the Ksa's warning, Bingo Loco reportedly converted its events into music quizzes to avoid gambling regulations. This adaptation indicates the immediate industry reaction to regulatory pressure, shifting the nature of the events to comply with non-gambling classifications.
