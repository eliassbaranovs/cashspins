# Florida Attorney General Boosts Florida Illegal Gambling Enforcement 2026

Florida Attorney General James Uthmeier has announced plans to significantly increase **Florida illegal gambling enforcement 2026**, targeting unlicensed operations across the state. This intensified effort aims to protect Florida's regulated gambling market, specifically the Seminole Tribe Compact, and to enhance player safety by reducing illicit market activity.

## Key Details of Florida Illegal Gambling Enforcement 2026

The Attorney General's office intends to ramp up enforcement actions throughout 2026. A core aspect of this push involves legislative proposals, such as HB 189, which seeks to reclassify certain gambling violations as felonies, thereby increasing potential penalties for operators. The focus of these enforcement activities will largely be on unlicensed online gambling platforms, which operate outside state regulations. The ultimate goal is to create safer gaming conditions for players by directing them towards regulated systems that offer consumer protections absent in illicit markets.

## Context and Background

This regulatory crackdown is primarily driven by the state's commitment to safeguarding the Seminole Tribe Compact. The compact provides the Seminole Tribe of Florida with exclusive control over certain gambling operations, including sports betting, and is a significant revenue source for the state. Efforts to curb illegal gambling are supported by the Seminole Tribe, as it directly protects their compact and revenue stream from unauthorized competition.

Despite the Attorney General's clear intent, major legislative efforts to strengthen gambling laws faced challenges in the 2026 session, with some bills failing to pass. Proposed legislation, such as SB 1580, suggested misdemeanor charges for players engaging in illegal online gambling, while HB 189 aimed to increase operator penalties. Sweepstakes operators have already faced scrutiny, with some receiving subpoenas and restricting access in Florida. These operators have criticized legislative language that criminalizes certain online games of chance. Conversely, some veterans' groups expressed concerns that the broad language in proposed bills could unintentionally impact their charitable gaming activities, contributing to legislative gridlock.

Even without new legislation passing, the Attorney General's office and the Florida Gaming Control Commission (FGCC) are actively conducting sting operations. In 2025, the FGCC reported seizures totaling over $14.4 million and 6,700 illegal slot machines, underscoring the ongoing nature of the crackdown. The Seminole Tribe's compact itself has withstood recent legal challenges, with a state judge dismissing a lawsuit seeking to invalidate it, thus maintaining the tribe's control over the sports betting market.
