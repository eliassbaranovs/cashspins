# India Enforces Major Online Gambling Ban, Prohibiting Over 8,300 Offshore Websites

**NEW DELHI, INDIA** – India has prohibited 8,376 online betting and gambling websites, marking a significant escalation in its regulatory efforts against offshore and unregulated platforms. This **India Online Gambling Ban 2026**, officially announced on April 5, 2026, is part of a broader government initiative to tighten control over the country's burgeoning online gaming landscape. Despite these comprehensive measures, challenges persist in fully preventing access to these offshore operators.

## Key Details of the Prohibition

The Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) confirmed the blocking of 8,376 specific URLs associated with online betting and gambling. This action targets platforms operating without domestic licenses, which often cater to Indian players from outside the country's jurisdiction. The move aligns with a global trend of increased regulatory enforcement against unlicensed operators, as nations seek to protect consumers and ensure compliance with local laws.

## Context and Industry Repercussions

This substantial ban follows the implementation of the Promotion and Regulation of Online Gaming Act, 2025, which has significantly reshaped India's online gaming sector. The domestic industry reacted with "shell-shock" to the sudden enforcement, with many companies suspending real-money operations. This has led to widespread layoffs, significant restructuring, and a re-evaluation of India's regulatory risk by investors.

Industry experts and critics largely contend that the ban will not eradicate gambling but will instead funnel players towards illegal offshore sites, thereby fostering a black market. They highlight severe economic repercussions, including an estimated loss of 200,000 to 400,000 jobs, stalled sponsorships, and a detrimental impact on related industries such as advertising and fintech. Some industry leaders have challenged the law in court, arguing that it fails to adequately differentiate between games of skill and games of chance. Furthermore, an ongoing dispute concerns the 28% Goods and Services Tax (GST) levied on the full face value of bets, which gaming companies are actively contesting.


## What This Means for Players

For individual players, the current legislation focuses enforcement efforts on operators, meaning players participating in money-based online games do not face direct penalties. However, a significant consequence is the observed migration to unregulated offshore sites. These platforms present considerable risks due to their lack of oversight and legal recourse. Players engaging with offshore sites face potential financial losses, fraud, frozen withdrawals, data theft, and the misuse of financial details. Without a domestic license, these operators are not subject to Indian consumer protection laws, leaving players vulnerable.

Studies indicate that the usage of illegal offshore websites has increased since the ban, with users reportedly spending longer periods and increasing their monthly expenditures on these platforms. Players are employing various methods to circumvent restrictions, such as using Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), mirror domains, and proxy credit cards. Domestically, the ban has also prompted some platforms to pivot towards offering non-monetary games or games with prizes instead of cash, to comply with the new regulatory framework.

## What's Next for India's Gambling Regulation

The Indian government continues to intensify its enforcement efforts. Recent actions include the blocking of an additional 242 to 300 illegal betting and gambling websites, bringing the total number of prohibited sites to nearly 8,400. This ongoing vigilance underscores the government's commitment to curbing unregulated online gambling.


Concerns have also been raised regarding the potential for offshore betting applications to engage in unauthorized data collection on Indian users, adding another layer of risk for players. In parallel with the ban, the government is actively promoting esports and social online games that do not involve money wagering, aiming to redirect player engagement towards regulated and safer entertainment options. The official Indian Premier League (IPL) fantasy league is reportedly struggling to attract users, facing stiff competition from illegal offshore platforms that continue to offer real-money contests, highlighting the persistent challenge of enforcement.
