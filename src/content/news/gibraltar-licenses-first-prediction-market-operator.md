# Gibraltar Issues First Prediction Market License 2026, Expanding iGaming Offerings

Gibraltar has granted its inaugural prediction market license to Predict Street Ltd on April 1, 2026, a move that significantly broadens the jurisdiction's iGaming offerings and regulatory scope. This **Gibraltar Prediction Market License 2026** introduces a new category of online betting, allowing users to wager on future event outcomes, and positions Gibraltar as a progressive regulator in the evolving digital gambling landscape. The decision marks a strategic expansion for the territory, aiming to attract new operators and players to its jurisdiction.

## Key Details of Gibraltar's Prediction Market License 2026

Prediction markets enable participants to bet on the outcome of future events, distinct from traditional sports betting or casino games. Predict Street Ltd's platform will initially focus on events such as the 2026 FIFA World Cup. A notable feature of the platform is its crypto-based nature, which may appeal to players interested in using digital currencies for wagering. The license was issued under Gibraltar's Gambling Act 2005, ensuring the operator adheres to established regulatory standards within this specialized betting niche.

## Strategic Context and Industry Reaction

The licensing decision underscores Gibraltar's progressive stance on online gambling innovation. Minister Nigel Feetham, Gibraltar's Minister for Justice, Trade and Industry, expressed strong optimism, anticipating that prediction markets will become a "substantial area of growth" for the territory. He highlighted the speed of the licensing process as evidence of Gibraltar's adaptable regulatory framework.

This move comes as Gibraltar's iGaming sector faces financial pressure from increased UK Remote Gaming Duty and Remote Betting Duty. In response, Gibraltar has been actively seeking new revenue streams and diversifying its economy, including exploring other fintech opportunities like crypto exchanges and digital asset management firms. The passing of the new Gambling Act 2025, although not yet implemented, further indicates a broader legislative update in Gibraltar's iGaming landscape. Minister Feetham has reportedly met with other potential prediction market operators, suggesting more licenses in this sector could be forthcoming.

Internationally, Malta's Gaming Authority has indicated keen interest in learning from Gibraltar's experience as it develops its own prediction market regulations. However, the move is viewed as potentially controversial by some, as other European regulators are considering banning or heavily restricting such platforms. There is also an acknowledgment within the industry that the overall market size and demand for prediction markets remain largely unproven, with Predict Street not yet having released user or revenue data. Globally, the regulatory status of prediction markets remains a complex and debated topic, with jurisdictions grappling with their classification as gambling, financial derivatives, or a distinct category.
