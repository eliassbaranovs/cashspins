# Brazil Online Gambling Decree May 2026: New Restrictions on Access and Advertising

Brazil is set to introduce a decree in May 2026 that will restrict online gambling access and advertising, signaling a tightening of regulations in the country's newly regulated iGaming market. This move reflects a hardening political discourse towards the sector and will impact how operators market their services and how players are exposed to gambling content.

## Key Details of Brazil's Online Gambling Decree May 2026

Brazil will introduce a decree in May 2026 aimed at restricting online gambling access and advertising. This legislative action signifies a more stringent regulatory environment for the iGaming market. The decree is a direct response to a hardening of political discourse surrounding the gambling sector within Brazil.

For operators, this means a necessary re-evaluation of their marketing strategies and methods for reaching players. Player exposure to gambling content will likely decrease. The regulated online gambling sector in Brazil launched in January 2025 and has since expanded significantly, now encompassing over 80 licensed operators. This rapid growth has occurred within a dynamic regulatory scene.

## Context: Brazil iGaming Regulation and Political Discourse

A hardening of political discourse towards the online gambling sector is evident in Brazil, leading to the impending decree. Licensed online betting operators have expressed surprise and concern regarding President Lula's statements, which hinted at increased restrictions or even a ban. Industry representatives have criticized these remarks as "prejudiced, uninformed and irresponsible," warning that a total ban could push players towards the unregulated black market. The Secretariat of Prizes and Betting (SPA) shares this concern, recognizing that a complete advertising ban might have the "reverse effect" of driving consumers to illegal operators.

This decree follows several legislative efforts. In February 2026, a Senate committee advanced a bill proposing a nationwide prohibition on sports betting and online game advertising. Sponsorships would also be prohibited. Following this, in March 2026, Congresswoman Tabata Amaral introduced Bill 1172/2026, which seeks a complete ban on advertising fixed-odds betting across all digital platforms. Additionally, the Workers' Party in Brazil has put forward Bill PL 1808/2026. This bill aims to repeal the existing 'Bets Law' and implement a comprehensive federal ban on online wagering. The consistent emergence of such bills indicates a sustained political push for stricter controls, a trend observed by industry analysts with five years of experience tracking regulatory shifts in emerging markets.

## Impact on Players: Brazil Gambling Access Restrictions

The upcoming decree will directly impact online casino players through specific access and advertising restrictions. Financially vulnerable individuals enrolled in federal debt renegotiation programs will be banned from accessing online betting platforms. This measure aims to protect consumers from further indebtedness.

Players will also experience a significant reduction in exposure to gambling advertising, particularly manipulative marketing tactics. Operators will face mandates to include messages on their proprietary channels that discourage gambling and warn of potential financial losses. These communications must also provide information aimed at preventing compulsive gambling. Such measures are intended to protect consumers and mitigate issues such as problem gambling and excessive debt.
