# FanDuel Michigan Multi-State Poker 2026 Approved by Regulator

The Michigan Gaming Control Board (MGCB) approved FanDuel for multi-state online poker play on April 3, 2026, enabling Michigan players to compete against opponents in other regulated US states. This significant regulatory decision is set to boost online poker liquidity and enhance the overall experience for FanDuel users.

This approval means players in Michigan can now join shared player pools with users in other jurisdictions where FanDuel's online poker product operates, such as Pennsylvania and New Jersey. The integration is expected to lead to larger prize pools, increased game availability, and a wider variety of stakes and formats for players.

## Key Details of the Approval

The MGCB's authorization specifically permits FanDuel to integrate its online poker platform in Michigan with those in other states that are part of multi-state agreements. This move aligns with a broader industry trend of consolidating player bases to create more robust and attractive online poker environments.

Michigan players previously had access to in-state only poker pools on FanDuel. With this change, the online poker experience is significantly expanded. The MGCB emphasized that FanDuel is a well-established operator in Michigan, and the integrity of the games will be maintained under the same rigorous oversight applied to all licensed gaming activity in the state.

## Context and Industry Landscape

This approval for FanDuel Michigan multi-state poker comes amidst several strategic developments in the US regulated online poker market. On April 1, 2026, PokerStars completed its transition to the FanDuel Casino platform, aligning with Flutter Entertainment's overarching strategy for its US operations. This consolidation aims to streamline offerings and maximize shared liquidity across their brands.

Prior to FanDuel's approval, other major operators had already made strides in multi-state poker. BetMGM Poker successfully merged its player pools across Pennsylvania, New Jersey, and Michigan on April 28, 2025. Similarly, WSOP Online integrated Michigan into its shared liquidity pool in May 2024. These integrations highlight the industry's continued drive towards a more interconnected US online poker ecosystem.

The Michigan Gaming Control Board views this authorization as a significant step forward in the development of online gaming within the state. MGCB Executive Director Henry Williams stated, "FanDuel is a well-established operator in the Michigan gaming market. Michigan players can have confidence in the integrity of the games they play, backed by the same rigorous oversight the MGCB applies across all licensed gaming activity." He added that the move reinforces the MGCB's commitment to legal, regulated gaming and responsible practices.

Further expansion of multi-state online poker in the US is also under consideration, with West Virginia having joined the Multi-State Internet Gaming Agreement (MSIGA) in 2023. Connecticut is actively exploring joining MSIGA, with relevant legislation proposed in March 2025. These developments underscore the growing momentum for shared poker liquidity across state lines.


## What This Means for Players

For online poker players in Michigan, the approval of FanDuel for multi-state play offers immediate and tangible benefits. Access to larger player pools from states like Pennsylvania and New Jersey directly translates to:

*   **Larger Tournament Prize Pools:** More participants mean bigger guarantees and more substantial payouts for top finishers in tournaments.
*   **Increased Game Availability:** Players will find a greater selection of cash games and tournaments running at various stakes throughout the day, reducing wait times and offering more options.
*   **Diverse Competition:** A broader player base introduces a wider range of playing styles and skill levels, enhancing the competitive experience.
*   **Variety of Stakes and Formats:** The increased liquidity supports a more diverse offering of poker variants and buy-in levels, catering to both casual players and high rollers.

This enhanced online poker experience on FanDuel is expected to increase player engagement and potentially attract new users to the platform in Michigan.

## What's Next

The integration of FanDuel's Michigan poker platform with other regulated states is expected to proceed swiftly following this approval. The industry anticipates continued efforts by regulators and operators to further integrate US online poker markets, potentially including additional states as they pass relevant legislation and join MSIGA. The MGCB's consistent approach to oversight ensures that as the market expands, player protection and game integrity remain paramount.
