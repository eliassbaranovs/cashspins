bio: |
  Ryan Torres is a gambling industry reporter who came to the beat sideways — he spent three years covering fintech and payments at a business publication before realising that the most interesting regulatory battles, the biggest money flows, and the most aggressive product innovation were all happening in online gambling. He made the switch and hasn't looked back. His journalism background shows in the way he structures a story: lead with the most important fact, explain why it matters, give the reader enough context to form their own opinion, and get out. He doesn't pad articles with filler paragraphs restating the obvious, and he doesn't hedge when he has a clear take. When a major operator pulls out of a market or a regulator announces new deposit limits, Ryan is the one explaining what it actually means for players — not just what the press release says, but what the second-order effects look like six months from now. He's particularly good at connecting dots between regulatory moves in different jurisdictions, tracking how a policy change in the UK often signals what's coming in other markets. His writing sits somewhere between a tech blog and a financial news desk: informed, slightly opinionated, accessible without being dumbed down. He covers platform launches, licensing changes, payment method shifts, and the occasional deep dive into how gambling companies actually make their money. Before his fintech career he studied journalism at Northwestern, which gave him the reporting instincts but not, he'll tell you, the patience for press embargoes.
